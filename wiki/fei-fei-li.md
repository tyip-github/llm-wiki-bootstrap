# Fei-Fei Li

**Summary**: Chinese-born American computer scientist, Stanford professor, and creator of ImageNet. Karpathy's PhD advisor and a key figure in the deep learning revolution.

## Background

Fei-Fei Li (born July 3, 1976, Beijing) is a computer scientist best known for establishing ImageNet, the dataset that enabled rapid advances in computer vision in the 2010s. She is a professor of computer science at Stanford University (source: Fei-Fei Li.md).

## Connection to LLM Wiki

Li was [[karpathy-bio|Andrej Karpathy's]] PhD advisor at the Stanford Vision Lab (2011–2015). She supervised his doctoral work on convolutional/recurrent neural networks for computer vision and NLP (source: Fei-Fei Li.md).

## Career

- **Stanford University** (2009–present) — professor of computer science; co-director of Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI) and Stanford Vision and Learning Lab (source: Fei-Fei Li.md)
- **Google Cloud** (2017–2018) — Chief Scientist of AI/ML, focused on democratizing AI (source: Fei-Fei Li.md)
- **Stanford AI Lab** (2013–2018) — director of SAIL (source: Fei-Fei Li.md)
- **World Labs** (2024–present) — co-founded startup for "spatial intelligence" AI; raised $1B by 2026 (source: Fei-Fei Li.md)

## Key contributions

- **ImageNet** — led development starting in 2007 at Princeton; over 14 million images across 22,000 categories, labeled using Amazon Mechanical Turk. The ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC, 2010–2017) catalyzed progress in deep learning (source: Fei-Fei Li.md)
- **CS231n** — the Stanford deep learning for computer vision course, originally designed and taught by Karpathy under Li's supervision (source: Fei-Fei Li.md)
- **AI4ALL** — co-founded nonprofit in 2017 (with Melinda French Gates and Jensen Huang) to increase diversity in AI (source: Fei-Fei Li.md)
