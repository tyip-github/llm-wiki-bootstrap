# Andrej Karpathy

**Summary**: AI researcher and educator who originated the LLM Wiki pattern. Founding member of OpenAI, former Director of AI at Tesla, and creator of Stanford's CS231n.

**Sources**: Andrej Karpathy 1.md, Andrej Karpathy.md, llm-wiki.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

## Background

Andrej Karpathy (born 23 October 1986) is a Slovak-Canadian AI researcher and educator (source: Andrej Karpathy.md). He was born in Bratislava, Czechoslovakia (now Slovakia) and moved to Toronto at age 15 (source: Andrej Karpathy.md). He is the originator of the [[llm-wiki-pattern]], shared as a gist designed to be copy-pasted into LLM agents (source: llm-wiki.md).

## Career

- **Eureka Labs** (2024–present) — founded an AI education platform; first course is LLM101n (source: Andrej Karpathy.md)
- **OpenAI** (2015–2017, 2023–2024) — founding member; later returned to build a team working on midtraining and synthetic data generation (source: Andrej Karpathy 1.md, Andrej Karpathy.md)
- **Tesla** (2017–2022) — Director of AI, leading the computer vision team for Tesla Autopilot and briefly Tesla Optimus. Reported to Elon Musk (source: Andrej Karpathy 1.md, Andrej Karpathy.md)
- **Stanford** (2011–2015) — PhD under [[fei-fei-li|Fei-Fei Li]] at the Stanford Vision Lab, focused on convolutional/recurrent neural networks for computer vision and NLP. Designed and taught CS231n, which grew from 150 to 750 students (source: Andrej Karpathy 1.md, Andrej Karpathy.md)

## Education

- BSc, University of Toronto (computer science and physics, minor in math) — first exposure to deep learning via Geoff Hinton's class (source: Andrej Karpathy 1.md)
- MSc, University of British Columbia — machine learning for physically-simulated robotics (source: Andrej Karpathy 1.md)
- PhD, Stanford University (source: Andrej Karpathy 1.md)

## Recognition

- MIT Technology Review Innovators Under 35 (2020) (source: Andrej Karpathy.md)
- Time Magazine 100 Most Influential People in AI (2024) (source: Andrej Karpathy.md)

## Notable contributions

- Coined the term **vibe coding** (February 2025) to describe how AI tools allow hobbyists to construct apps by typing prompts (source: Andrej Karpathy.md)
- Early YouTube channel **badmephisto** with Rubik's cube tutorials (9M+ views), used by speedcubers including Feliks Zemdegs (source: Andrej Karpathy.md)
