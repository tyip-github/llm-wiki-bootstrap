# Memex

**Summary**: Vannevar Bush's 1945 concept of a personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails — a precursor to the LLM Wiki pattern.

**Sources**: llm-wiki.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## Background

The Memex was a hypothetical device described by Vannevar Bush in his 1945 essay "As We May Think." It envisioned a personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents (source: llm-wiki.md).

## Connection to LLM Wiki

The [[llm-wiki-pattern]] is related in spirit to the Memex. Bush's vision was closer to the LLM Wiki than to what the web became: **private, actively curated, with the connections between documents as valuable as the documents themselves** (source: llm-wiki.md).

The part Bush could not solve was who does the maintenance. The LLM handles that (source: llm-wiki.md).

## Key parallels

The following table synthesizes the parallels between Bush's Memex and the modern LLM Wiki, based on the characteristics described in the original gist (source: llm-wiki.md):

| Memex (1945) | LLM Wiki (2026) |
|---|---|
| Personal knowledge store | Personal wiki in a git repo |
| Associative trails | Wiki-links and cross-references |
| Human-curated | Human-curated sources, LLM-maintained wiki |
| Maintenance unsolved | LLM handles maintenance |
